Singapore and Zurich are now more expensive than New York, according to the EIU Index.

The index analyzes prices for things goods and services in 173 world cities globally, for example, transport, rent, food, and alcohol. Geneva, New York, and Hong Kong are among the top five most expensive cities. Global prices increased by 7.4%, which was a little less than in the previous year. Food prices continued to rise, while strong demand for traveling after the pandemic meant an increase in transport prices.

In 2024, experts expect higher interest rates, less business activity, and consumer demand.
